The Story

When your father is a champion, whose game are you really playing?

Eleven-year-old Nikko loves soccer. He loves the sound of a clean pass, the feel of cool grass under his cleats, the way the ball seems to think along with him.

He also loves his father — a champion in a different sport, a man whose shadow stretches across every field Nikko plays on. And somewhere deep inside, Nikko carries a question he has been afraid to ask:

Do I love soccer because I love it — or because I'm trying to become something my dad already is?

When his quiet coach takes Nikko to an abandoned field at the top of a hill — no bleachers, no scoreboard, no one watching — Nikko begins a journey that will teach him everything his trophies never could.

That the ball is a partner, not a thing to master. That the mean voice in his head is not the truth. That the teammate who bothers him most has brought him a mirror. And that honoring his father has nothing to do with becoming him.

Meet the Authors

A father and son, writing from opposite ends of the same journey.

Herb Perez
Olympic Gold Medalist • Author • Coach

Herb Perez won the only Gold Medal awarded in Taekwondo at the 1992 Barcelona Olympic Games. A 9th Degree Black Belt and seven-time National Champion, he holds a Juris Doctorate from Rutgers Law School and a Master's Degree in Sport Organization Management from the IOC's program at the University of Lyon, France.

He is the author of The Complete Tae Kwon Do for Kids, One Champion's Pathway, and The Mountain: A Story About Finding Yourself, and serves as head instructor of Gold Medal Martial Arts in California.

Nikko Perez
Soccer Player • First-Time Author

Nikko Perez began playing soccer at age four and has worked his way through every level of the youth game since. He developed with MLS Next club Silicon Valley Soccer Academy under Division I coach Chuy Sanchez.

Scouted in his junior year by numerous D1 programs, he committed to Cornell University under Head Coach John Smith. Most recently, he was recruited by the San Jose Earthquakes Academy U18 team — MLS's highest level of youth development — under Coach Alex Covelo and Academy Director Luchi Gonzalez.

The Field is his first book.
